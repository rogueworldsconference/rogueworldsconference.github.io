Scientific Rationale

Free-floating, or "rogue", planetary-mass objects have been discovered wandering through the Galaxy unbound to any star. The origins of these objects remain poorly understood, and likely involve a combination of many different processes relevant to star and planet formation.

Direct imaging surveys of young star-forming regions continue to find hundreds of high-mass rogue planets, though it remains an ongoing theoretical challenge to determine what fraction represent ejected planets as opposed to aborted stellar embryos. Meanwhile, upcoming space-based microlensing programs, such as the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope, are poised to vastly increase our sample of free-floating planets at masses extending to Earth-mass and below, opening a new window into their origin and demographics.

Following the success of previous workshops, Rogue Worlds 3 is a 4-day workshop designed to bring together researchers at the forefront of different aspects of the study of free-floating planets. Key focal areas include:

  • Microlensing: Ground- and space-based detections, demographics, and mass functions.
  • Direct Imaging: Observational breakthroughs, characterization of young planetary-mass objects, and atmospheric modeling.
  • Dynamics: Modeling planetary ejection mechanisms, stellar cluster dynamics, and the fate of outer planetary systems.
  • Moons Orbiting Rogue Worlds: Theoretical constraints, formation mechanics, and potential detection channels for exomoons bound to free-floating hosts.

Venue & Logistics

The conference will take place in the Phillips Auditorium at the Center for Astrophysics | Harvard & Smithsonian in Cambridge, Massachusetts.

There will NOT be a remote option to attend the conference. However, if travel poses a significant barrier, please reach out to the LOC.

The Venue

Phillips Auditorium
Center for Astrophysics
60 Garden Street
Cambridge, MA 02138

Phillips Auditorium is located in Building D of the historic 60 Garden Street observatory complex.

Travel: Cambridge, MA is conveniently connected to the major transportation infrastructure in Boston, MA.

Airport: Boston Logan International Airport is a 20 minute taxi/rideshare to the Center for Astrophysics, or take the free bus to South Station, and then connect to the Red Line (towards Alewife) and exit at Harvard Square.

Train: South Station is also a major hub for train travel with convenient routes along the Eastern Seaboard of the USA through Amtrak.

Transit: The Center for Astrophysics is accessible via a 15-minute walk from the Harvard Square MBTA station (Red Line). Frequent local bus routes (MBTA 74, 75, and 78) also connect Harvard Square Station with the Center for Astrophysics (take the stop at Concord Ave opppsote Buckingham St).

Code of Conduct

The conference organizers are committed to making this meeting productive and enjoyable for everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, nationality, or religion. Harassment of participants in any form will not be tolerated.

To participate at the Rogue Worlds 3 Conference, please follow these guidelines:

  • Professional Behavior: Harassment and sexist, racist, or exclusionary comments or jokes are not appropriate. Harassment includes sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, sexual attention or innuendo, deliberate intimidation, stalking, and photography or recording of an individual without consent. It also includes offensive comments related to gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion.
  • Audience Awareness: All communication should be appropriate for a professional audience including people of many different backgrounds. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate.
  • Respectful Critique: Be respectful and do not insult or put down other attendees or facilitators of the event. Critique ideas, not people.
  • Social Media & Sharing: If participants wish to share photos or contents of talks/slides of any attendee or speaker on social media, we ask that they first get permission.
  • Enforcement: Participants asked to stop any inappropriate behavior are expected to comply immediately. Attendees violating these rules may be asked to leave the event at the sole discretion of the organizers without a refund of any charge.

This code of conduct is based on the "London Code of Conduct," as originally designed for the conference "Accurate Astrophysics. Correct Cosmology," held in London in July 2015.
